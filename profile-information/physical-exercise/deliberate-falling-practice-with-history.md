# Deliberate falling practice (with history)

## Goal and functional relevance

The goal of this practice is to maintain and verify my ability to fall
the "right" way, using large surface areas to reduce the pressure at
impact, and reduce the risk of hurting joints. The reason this is
important is that accidental falls continue to be a risk.

The verification is both that I use the correct way of falling, and
that my level of apparent physical injury and perceived mental injury
is low enough. That not only verifies the correctness of my method of
falling, but also provides weak evidence of bone, muscle, joint, and
skin health.

Reference videos:

* [How to Fall Without Injury](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o_he-KVCIkY)
* [How to Fall Safely](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZVzzJ4xDgoE)

## Current protocol

* Catch-myself-before-falling exercise, where my goal is to
  simulate tripping while leaning very far forward, and yet catch
  myself.

* Fall-forward onto exercise mat, with my arm on my side, clasped
  together, or behind my back (so that they are not "ready" to be in
  the correct position). This not only tests that I remember the
  correct way of falling on my forearms, but also checks that I can
  quickly move my arms into correct position even as I am falling
  down.

Things to check after:

* Process: I should take the impact on my forearms and maybe my knees.

* Results: I should have no physical pain, no visible injury, and no
  deterioration of strength/function for the strength exercises I do
  immediately afterward.

## Triggers for overall exercise

I do this exercise as part of my [non-strength day exercise
cycle](non-strength-day-exercise-cycle-with-history.md). If I am doing
exercise every day and not skipping it, it should happen once every 9
days. In practice, it happens every 15 days or so.

## History

### 2024-10-05: initiation of falling forward exercise

I started the exercise on 2024-10-05. At the time of starting it, I
tended to do it right after strength exercises (weightlifting and
push-ups), since I already had the exercise mat out, and also based on
the view that it would be a corrective to the "hands-out" motor
pattern that push-ups train that is counterproductive for falls.

Unfortunately, I failed to adhere to the weekly cadence, partly due to
forgetfulness and partly because of the unpleasantness of the
exercise.

### 2026-03-24: switch to non-strength days

On 2026-03-24, based on a [discussion with
Claude](https://claude.ai/chat/b876a5f7-97d0-40f9-b5ac-7e3708b6e1a0),
I decided to switch from doing deliberate falling practice right after
strength exercises (weightlifting and push-ups) to doing them on
non-strength exercise days. The main reason was Claude's statement
that motor patterns don't cancel out through sequential activation,
and that the fatigue from the strength exercises might be contributing
to the unpleasantness of the deliberate falling practice right after
-- which in turn meant I did it less often, and therby got less
benefit.

Based on this, I decided to switch the falling practice timing to
non-strength days, and added it to the de facto [non-strength day
exercise cycle](non-strength-day-exercise-cycle-with-history.md), even
though I would get the idea to formulate the cycle explicitly only on
2026-04-10, and actually document the cycle on 2026-05-04.
