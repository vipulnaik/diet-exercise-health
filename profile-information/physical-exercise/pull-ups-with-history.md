# Pull-ups (with history)

Reference article: https://outlift.com/chin-ups-vs-pull-ups/

## Goal and functional relevance of exercise

### Build towards being able to consistently do 5 underhand and 5 overhand pull-ups when doing the exercise after dead hang

The goal of pull-ups is to improve upper body strength, particularly
arm and hand strength. It complements the [dead
hang](dead-hang-with-history.md). My goal is to do the pull-ups after
the dead hang exercises.

## Current protocol

After finishing the overhand and underhand dead hang, I do the
overhand and underhand pull-ups. Although my original intent was to
vary the order between overhand and underhand, in practice I have been
doing overhand before underhand. Within each, I do the pull-ups in
series (i.e., the count measures the number of pull-ups I do in
succession while hanging).

Currently, when it's done after the dead hang, and with overhand
before underhand, I can do about 4 to 7 overhand pull-ups and 3 to 5
underhand pull-ups.

## History

### 2024-11-14

I started doing pull-ups regularly (once evert 2-3 days, after dead
hang) on 2024-11-14.

As of this point, when done after the dead hang, I could do about 2-3
underhand and 2-3 overhand.

### 2025-01-26 status update

As of 2025-01-26, if I do the pull-ups immediately after doing an
overhand and an underhand dead hang, and I start with overhand
pull-ups, I can do 4 to 5 overhand pull-ups and 3 to 4 underhand
pull-ups.

### 2026-03-25 status update

A continued challenge with increasing pull-ups is that I do them after
dead hang exhaustion, so even as my overall strength improves, the
residual strength after dead hang improves less than proportionately.

My ranges have improved slightly, but more on the upper end than the
lower end:

* Overhand: (4 to 5) to (4 to 7) -- indicating more ability to
  occasionally do more pull-ups, but no change to the level I can
  consistently achieve across days

* Underhand: (3 to 4) to (3 to 5) -- indicating occasional ability to
  get to 5 pull-ups, but no change to the level I can consistently
  achieve across days
